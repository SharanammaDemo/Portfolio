    Lab 1 Details

    Image and Video Processing Laboratory 1

    Description: Completed a laboratory exercise focusing on the basics of image processing. This involved reading, displaying, and saving images in various file formats.

    Technologies Used: Python, OpenCV

    Key Learnings:

    • Techniques for loading and saving images.
    • Basic image manipulation and understanding image formats.
    • In our first lab session on Image and Video Processing, delved into a variety of tasks. Explored the fundamental processes of reading, displaying, and saving images to different drives. It was intriguing to manipulate images by changing the center pixel and transforming the top left corner into a uniform blue color. Additionally, delved into converting images to grayscale, adding text overlays, drawing circles, and embedding text within those circles. Furthermore, practiced converting RGB color models to other models like CMY with the suffix K and YCBCR with the suffix R. These hands-on activities provided a practical understanding of essential image processing techniques and tools. The lab exercises offered a comprehensive introduction to the intricacies of image processing. I gained valuable experience in handling images effectively, from basic tasks like reading and displaying images to more advanced manipulations such as altering specific pixels and colors. By converting images to grayscale, adding text elements, and drawing shapes like circles, honed our skills in image editing. Moreover, the exploration of color model conversions, such as RGB to CMY with the suffix K and YCBCR with the suffix R, expanded our understanding of color representation in digital images. Overall, these practical tasks equipped us with a solid foundation in image processing methodologies and techniques.
